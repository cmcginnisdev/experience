Note Card 1

Main Point: Agile Manifesto prioritizes individuals and interactions over processes and tools, working software over comprehensive documentation, customer collaboration over contract negotiation, and responding to change over following a plan.
Details: It was codified in 2001 in Snowbird by Scrum, XP, and DSDM practitioners. The values of the Agile Manifesto are fundamental to the success of agile methodologies in modern project management.

Note Card 2

Main Point: Sprint basics include Sprint Planning, Sprint Development, and Sprint Retro & Review, with a Sprint being a timebox for completing work, usually ranging from two weeks to a month.
Details: Sprint Planning involves selecting work from the Product Backlog, Sprint Development focuses on daily reporting and execution against selected stories, and Sprint Retro & Review are crucial for continuous improvement.

Note Card 3

Main Point: The Iron Triangle explains project management methods, including Agile, Traditional, and Lean, each with varying constraints on scope, schedule, and budget.
Details: All aspects of the Iron Triangle - scope, schedule, and budget - are constraints and costs to the organization. Understanding these constraints is crucial for effective project management.

Note Card 4

Main Point: Agile focuses on speed and flexibility, Traditional on efficiency, and Lean on innovation, with each method requiring specific goals and expertise.
Details: Agile projects require a shared vision, whole teams, incremental delivery, continuous integration & testing, and frameworks like Scrum, SAFe, or Disciplined Agile for successful implementation.

Note Card 5

Main Point: Every project must have a charter, plan, documentation, design, and testing to be successful.
Details: Agile methodologies prioritize varying scope to target what the customer needs, thus reducing waste by delivering a minimum viable product (MVP) that meets project objectives.
